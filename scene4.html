India Ground Water Levels

Every year, the Central Ground Water Board(CGWB) measures level of water in wells located across India to understand the depletion happening due to consumption and recharge happening due to rainfall. Based on the ratio of recharge and extraction(also called as ground water development), they mark the block/zone as safe, semi-critical, critical, or overexploited. This data helps government form programs and policies targeting the overexploited areas.
